Cappuccino

The term "cappuccino" comes from the Italian word "cappuccio," which means "hood" or "cap." The "ino" suffix makes it diminutive, so "cappuccino" essentially means "small cap."

Espresso

A concentrated coffee drink brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans. The perfect base for many other coffee drinks.

Latte

A coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk. The word "latte" comes from the Italian "caff√® latte," which means "milk coffee."

Americano

A type of coffee drink prepared by diluting an espresso with hot water, giving it a similar strength to, but different flavor from, traditionally brewed coffee.

Macchiato

An espresso coffee drink with a small amount of milk, usually foamed. The word "macchiato" means "spotted" or "stained" in Italian.

Mocha

A chocolate-flavored coffee drink that is a variant of a latte. It consists of espresso, hot milk, and chocolate syrup or powder.

Flat White

A coffee drink consisting of espresso with microfoam (steamed milk with small, fine bubbles). It originated in Australia and New Zealand.

Cortado

A Spanish coffee drink consisting of espresso mixed with a roughly equal amount of warm milk to reduce the acidity. The word "cortado" means "cut" in Spanish.

Affogato

An Italian coffee-based dessert consisting of a scoop of vanilla ice cream "drowned" in a shot of hot espresso. Perfect for dessert lovers!
